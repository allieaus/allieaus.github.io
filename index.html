I'm a PhD student at the University of California, Davis. I currently work with Dr. Kwan-Liu Ma at the VIDI Labs. I previously worked with Dr. Bo Fu in the D2 Lab at California State University, Long Beach.

Research topics I am currently interested in are visual analytics for explainable AI and monitoring high-performance computing (HPC) systems.


On-going Projects

Visual Analytics Approach for Evaluating LLM-Generated Protein Interaction Predictions

Molecular data has been widely used to enrich visual representations of protein-protein interaction (PPI) networks, and has been expanded to support large-language models (LLMs) for various tasks (e.g. protein generation, interaction prediction). I am exploring methods for evaluating and visualizing accuracy and feasibility of novel PPIs generated by LLMs. [code][poster]

Visualizing Distributed Resilient Workflows and Infrastructure

With the use of HPC systems for large-scale experiments and simulations, visual analytics tools provide feasible monitoring solutions for many HPC applications. I am currently exploring functional data analysis (FDA) and AI/ML solutions for capturing real-time performance of HPC monitoring data for visualization. [details]

Past Projects

LLMs for Anomalous HPC Log Sequence Prediction

Due to their popularity, transformer-based models, particularly large-language models (LLMS), have led the most recent efforts to predict anomalies in HPC system logs. We compared variations of GPT-2 and BERT models on predicting failure sequences in BlueGene/L supercomputer and System 20 cluster logs. [paper][slides]
