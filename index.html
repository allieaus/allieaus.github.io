I'm a PhD student at the University of California, Davis. I currently work with Dr. Kwan-Liu Ma at the VIDI Labs. I previously worked with Dr. Bo Fu in the D2 Lab at California State University, Long Beach.

Research topics I am currently interested in are visual analytics, time series anomaly detection, and high-performance computing systems.


On-going Projects

Informative Visualization of HPC Performance

With the use of high-performance computing (HPC) systems for large-scale experiments and simulations, visual analytics tools provide feasible monitoring solutions for many HPC applications. However, these systems are becoming increasingly complex and require efficient analysis of unlabelled, high-resolution data. I am currently exploring functional data analysis (FDA) techniques for streaming visualization of HPC performance data.

LLMs for HPC Log Sequence Prediction

Due to their popularity, transformer-based models, particularly large-language models (LLMS), have led the most recent efforts to predict anomalies in HPC system logs. I explored the applications of LLMs in HPC log prediction by comparing variations of GPT-2 and BERT models on predicting failure sequences in BlueGene/L supercomputer and System 20 cluster logs.
